noun | / hɪˈrɛθ /

Ever present, looming, simmering under the surface. Increasing in clarity as time passes by but never really coming close to a revelation of what it actually is. Always just a little bit out of reach. In some senses it feels like an existential Shepard tone. Familiar and foreign at the same time, coming and going but never really dissipating. Perennial.

There were days when I would look out the windows in my pre-teen years, feeling what I would now call _ma_ (間) - the intervals, the in-betweens. Ghost notes on the drums, syncopation in jazz. In those moments I would first feel like the observer, as I usually would have, always beside myself, then I would slowly return and realise that I am living that moment and not just playing things out. This hyperawareness I suppose has always plagued me my whole life.
